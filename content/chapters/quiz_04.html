# `TableVectorizer`

## Question 1 
::: {.callout}
What is the proper way of setting the number of components used by the high 
cardinality encoder in the `TableVectorizer`? 

- [ ] A) By passing `n_components` as kwargs:
```{.python}
TableVectorizer(n_components=10)
```
- [ ] B) By creating a new encoder with the specified parameter:
```{.python}
new_encoder = StringEncoder(n_components=10)
TableVectorizer(high_cardinality=new_encoder)
```
- [ ] C) Both A) and B) are correct
- [ ] D) None of the above
:::


::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
Answer: B)
:::

## Question 2
::: {.callout}
The `TableVectorizer` categorizes columns based on their characteristics. What 
are the types of columns that it handles? 

- [ ] A) Numeric, categorical, datetimes
- [ ] B) Numeric, datetimes, high cardinality categorical, low cardinality categorical
- [ ] C) Integer, float, string, categorical, datetimes
- [ ] D) Numeric, datetimes, high cardinality categorical, low cardinality categorical, and 
complex objects such as structs or lists

:::

::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
Answer: B)

By default, the `TableVectorizer` converts complex objects to their string representation, 
before checking their cardinality. No special transformation is provided for columns
that contain structs or lists. 

:::

```{.python}
tabular_pipeline("regression")
tabular_pipeline("classification")
```

- [ ] A) `RidgeCV` and `ExtraTreeClassifier`
- [ ] B) `HistGradientBoostingRegressor` and `LogisticRegression`
- [ ] C) `RandomForestRegressor` and `RandomForestClassifier`
- [ ] D) `HistGradientBoostingRegressor` and `HistGradientBoosting`

:::

::: {.callout-tip collapse="true"}
Answer: D)
:::
